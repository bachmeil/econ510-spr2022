GDP Solution Example

Here’s the algebra example from the Jan 27 class. You were given the information:

  • The consumption function is \[C=100+0.9(Y-T)\]
  • Investment is 900
  • Government spending is 150 and there’s a balanced budget. That means taxes are 150. In practice, the budget doesn’t have to be balanced, and it usually isn’t. I could have given you some other amount for taxes, something that would be larger or smaller than 150. Since I told you the budget is balanced, you know taxes are 150.

This information is enough to solve for GDP. First, given the definition of demand: \[Z = C + I + G\] where - Z is demand - C is personal consumption - I is investment - G is government purchases of goods and services

Step 1. Plug into the definition to find demand as a function of income: \[Z = 100 + 0.9(Y - 150) + 900 + 150\] where we have pulled each of those numbers from the information given above. Then find demand as a function of income: \[Z = 1150 + 0.9Y - 135\] \[Z = 1015 + 0.9Y\]

Step 2. Use the equilibrium condition. Firms try to produce the amount that’s demanded over a reasonable period of time. That means we have the equilibrium condition \[Y=Z\]. Applying that condition by replacing Z with Y: \[Y = 1015 + 0.9Y\]

We have income/output (Y) on both sides of that equation. Solving for Y using algebra, we have \[Y = 1015 + 0.9Y\] \[(1 - 0.9)Y = 1015\] \[0.1Y = 1015\] \[Y^{*} = 10,150\]

Related. We made use of the concept that income and output are the same thing in the goods market model. There’s a short post on Canvas (see the Canvas landing page for a link or ask me if you don’t see it) clarifying this point.
