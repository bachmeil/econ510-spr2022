In-Class Exercise 4, Feb 24

After-tax income:

$100,000 when young
$200,000 when old

  1. Calculate LATI.

Answer: $100,000 + $200,000 = $300,000

  1. Calculate the PIH level of consumption when young and when old (two numbers). In other words, assume consumption smoothing.

Answer: $300,000/2 = $150,000. That is the amount they consume when both young and old.

  1. Calculate the amount of borrowing/repayment when young and when old (different answers for each).

Answer: Spending $150,000 when young requires borrowing $50,000 because income is only $100,000. The $50,000 will have to be repaid when old.

  1. Redo (a)-(c) if the government sends a check for $50,000 when young and increases taxes by $50,000 when old to pay for it.

LATI is unaffected. Income rises by $50,000 when young. Taxes rise by $50,000 when old.

Consumption is also unaffected. They have the same desired consumption as before, because LATI is not affected.

There will be no borrowing when young. There will be no repayment when old. The stimulus check substitutes for the need to borrow.

Extensions discussed in class:

Extension 1: Credit constraint

What happens in the above problem if there is a credit constraint that sets maximum borrowing at $10,000?

In the first case, this causes a liquidity constraint. The maximum level of consumption with the credit constraint is $110,000 when young. Since that’s less than optimal consumption, consumption is $110,000 when young and $190,000 when old.

In the second case, the credit constraint doesn’t make any difference. The stimulus check was a substitute for borrowing, so there was no need to borrow. Since it’s possible to achieve the optimal consumption when there’s a stimulus check, consumption continues to be $150,000 when young and $150,000 when old.

Extension 2: Stimulus check larger than needed

What happens if the stimulus check is actually $100,000 when young and taxes rise $100,000 when old?

It doesn’t make any difference for consumption whether the stimulus check is $50,000 or $100,000. $50,000 is sufficient to allow optimal consumption ($150,000 in each time period) so consumption is not affected. The extra $50,000 from the stimulus check is saved and used to pay $50,000 worth of taxes when old.
